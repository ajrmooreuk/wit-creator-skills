Every title must pass at least 2 of these 4 tests:

| Principle | Test | Example (Women in Tech) |

# SKILL 4: Script It — Hook, Help, Ask

Write a complete, ready-to-film video script. Every video follows the same pattern.

---

## The 5-Block Structure

### BLOCK 1 — HOOK (0–15 seconds)
Say something unexpected. Establish stakes. Preview the payoff.

> "Here's what nobody tells you about [problem]... [unexpected claim]. By the end of this video, you'll [specific outcome]."

### BLOCK 2 — PROBLEM (15–60 seconds)
Name the pain with specificity. Make them feel seen.

> "If you've ever [specific scenario], you're not alone. The reason this keeps happening is [root cause]."

### BLOCK 3 — SOLUTION (bulk of video)
Break into 3–5 clear, numbered steps. Each step: state it → explain it → show an example.

### BLOCK 4 — PROOF (30 seconds)
Show it works. Personal result, data point, or viewer testimonial. Be specific.

### BLOCK 5 — ASK (15 seconds)
ONE call to action only. Tie it to the value just delivered.

> "If this helped, subscribe — I put out a new video every [day]. And if you want the deeper version, I break this down step-by-step in my newsletter — link in the description."

---

## Write Your Script

```
VIDEO TITLE: [from Skill 3]
TARGET LENGTH: [Short 5-8 min / Medium 8-15 min / Long 15-25 min]

[0:00] HOOK
[Write your opening — read it aloud, must be under 15 seconds]

[0:15] PROBLEM
[Name the pain. Use a scenario your ideal viewer has lived.]

[1:00] SOLUTION
Step 1: [state it]
[explain it]
[example]
[B-ROLL: ...]

Step 2: [state it]
[explain it]
[example]
[SCREEN: ...]

Step 3: [state it]
[explain it]
[example]

[X:XX] PROOF
[Your result, data, or testimonial]

[X:XX] ASK
[One CTA only]
```

# SKILL 5: Shorts — Your Discovery Engine

Build a Shorts system that brings new viewers to your long-form channel.

---

## Shorts Rules

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| 30–45 seconds max | Completion rate is the algorithm signal |
| One idea per Short | Clarity beats density |
| Hook in first 2 seconds | "The biggest mistake in [topic]..." |
| End with channel reference | "Full breakdown on my channel" |
| Vertical 9:16 | Mobile-first |

---

## 3 Shorts Per Video

For every long-form video you publish, create 3 Shorts:

| Type | What It Is | When to Post |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Best Moment** | Strongest 30 seconds from your video | Same day as long-form |
| **Contrarian Hook** | Bold claim, no resolution — drives to full video | 2 days after |
| **Quick Win** | One actionable tip, self-contained | 4 days after |

---

# SKILL 6: Get Found — Simple SEO

Make every video searchable. This is a checklist, not a course.

---

## Keyword Check

| Question | Your Answer |
|----------|------------|
| What would your ideal viewer type into YouTube search? (primary keyword) | |
| 3–5 variations of that phrase? | |
| 2–3 question-format searches? ("how to...", "why does...", "best way to...") | |

**Demand test:** Search your primary keyword on YouTube.
- 3+ similar results → demand confirmed
- 0 results → nobody's searching — rethink the keyword
- 100+ results from big channels → too competitive — narrow it

---

## Pre-Upload SEO Checklist

Use this before **every upload**:

### Before Upload
- [ ] Primary keyword identified and demand-validated
- [ ] Title under 60 characters with keyword in first half
- [ ] Thumbnail ready

### During Upload
- [ ] Description: first 2 lines state what the video solves (include keyword naturally)
- [ ] Description: newsletter link included
- [ ] Description: timestamps/chapters added
- [ ] Tags: 5–8 tags (primary keyword, 2–3 variations, channel name, niche, broad category)
- [ ] Category: most relevant YouTube category selected
- [ ] Playlist: added to relevant playlist

### After Upload
- [ ] Pinned comment: question related to the video topic
- [ ] Community post or Substack Note announcing the video
- [ ] Shorts scheduled (from Skill 5 calendar)

### Weekly
- [ ] Check Analytics → Traffic sources → YouTube search
- [ ] Note which keywords drive views
- [ ] Update future ideas based on search data

**Output:** Save as `seo-checklist-v1.md`.

# SKILL 7: Build Your Community — 15 Minutes a Day

Build a loyal audience. This is a routine, not a strategy deck.

---

## Daily Routine (15 minutes)

| Block | Time | Action | Why |
|-------|------|--------|-----|
| Reply | 5 min | Reply to every new comment on your latest video. Ask a follow-up question in at least 1 reply. Heart/like every comment. | YouTube treats replies as engagement signals — boosts the video |
| Outreach | 5 min | Leave 3 thoughtful comments on channels your audience watches (from your Channel Brief competitor list). Add value — not "great video!" | Gets your name in front of the right people |
| Signal | 5 min | Share one useful thought on Substack Notes, LinkedIn, or X. Repurpose a key insight from your latest video. | Stays visible between uploads |

---

## Weekly Routine (30 minutes)

| Block | Time | Action | Why |
|-------|------|--------|-----|
| Collaborate | 15 min | DM one creator in your niche. Template: *"Hey [name], loved your video on [topic]. I'm working on [related thing] — would you be up for [specific collab idea]?"* Track in a list. Follow up once after 1 week. | Fastest subscriber growth lever |
| Listen | 15 min | Review this week's comments for recurring themes. Ask your audience what they want next (poll or question). Check Analytics → Audience → when your viewers are online. | Content they asked for always outperforms guesses |
